The Lost Art of Waiting: How Instant Delivery Changed Our Psychology

We can get groceries in 10 minutes and lovers with a swipe. But neuroscientists argue that the elimination of anticipation is altering our brain chemistry.

By Dr. Ananya Sharma 12 Min Read • Pg. 4
FILE PHOTO / TIMES ARCHIVES

There was a time, not so long ago, when waiting was a structured part of the human experience. You waited for the postman, you waited for your favorite song to play on the radio, and you waited for the Sunday paper to arrive to solve the crossword.

Today, algorithms have eradicated the pause. The '10-minute delivery' wars have conditioned us to view any delay as a service failure rather than a natural state of living. This immediate gratification cycle, researchers note, is severely depleting our dopamine reserves.
